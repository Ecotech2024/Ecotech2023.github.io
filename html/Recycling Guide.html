Reduce and Reuse:
• Make your devices last longer: Take care of your electronics with protective cases and regular cleaning. Update software and keep them running smoothly.
• Can it be repurposed? Could an old phone become an MP3 player? Can a tablet be used for e-reading? Get creative and extend the life of your gadgets!
• Donate to a good cause: Schools, libraries, and tech training programs can often use older electronics. Just be sure to wipe all personal data first!

Recycle Right:
• Find a certified e-waste recycler: Look online or ask at local electronics stores for drop-off locations. E-waste processors have the expertise to handle these materials safely and responsibly.
• Know what's accepted: Recycling programs may have specific guidelines about what they take. Be sure your e-waste is sorted correctly for the most effective processing.

Modern Recycling Techniques:
• Urban mining: E-waste is a treasure trove of valuable materials like gold, copper, and rare earth elements. New techniques extract these materials for reuse in new products.
• Bio-shredding: Some companies are shredding e-waste with the help of microbes that can break down certain components, making it easier to recover valuable parts.
